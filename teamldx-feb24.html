A new year, a new opportunity - reaching new heights with LDX


LDX is up and running as part of the TDX family, for this month's introduction we spoke with Joe Gradwell to get his thoughts about the new operation.

It's not every day you get the chance to develop a new business unit, but bringing the LDX and Linde partnership to life has been a fascinating, challenging - and ultimately rewarding - experience to be part of.

The launch of LDX had been brewing and started many months ago when Linde approached TDX to discuss a possible partnership. They admired the TDX values and thought they would be a great fit with Linde.

After many months of working on the agreement, the new deal was struck, and Linde AU is 100% committed to making our partnership work.

We're privileged to have a great team at LDX, we recently got everyone together for technical training and the calibre of our personnel was clear to see.

The TDX name is so highly regarded in the market, that there were more than 300 applicants for recently advertised roles (a big thanks must go to the recruitment team for their support through this process!). This demonstrates that TDX and LDX are viewed as desirable companies to work with, and we can attract high-performing candidates from within the industry. Simply, people value the TDX and LDX brands and are attracted to being part of our story.

Our brands are so renowned that, since the launch of LDX, we have received multiple approaches from significant potential customers who want to do business with us. The backing of the TDX brand adds credibility to what LDX can offer.

From a business perspective, the new LDX operation will also offer a significant advantage for TDX Group when it comes to cash flow. Material Handling/ Forklift rentals are generally five-year terms, which means income every month. After the long-term contracts finish, machines are then reallocated for shorter-term rentals.

The upshot is that there is a constant cash flow, which makes a significant and consistent contribution to the performance of the business - especially considering there are more than 300 forklifts on fully maintained service agreements currently in operation around the country which we will be picking up in the coming months from Linde AU which will provide immediate work for our teams.

I'd like to thank Colm for the opportunity to get LDX up and running and thank all the other people involved in bringing it to life. (Check the 'Hero a Staff Member' story for more details about someone pivotal to the project).

I'd also like to share a couple of exciting new additions to the LDX team.

Millie Smyth has accepted a full-time Administrator role with LDX. Millie had been working in our TDX Central Admin team for a wee while on a casual agreement, and then with the start of LDX there was an opportunity to join permanently. We are very lucky that Millie has accepted a full-time role with us, she brings with her a good understanding of TDX and how we operate as a company, and she will now assist us as we build up the newly formed LDX.

I'd also like to introduce John Shatford as the new LDX Service Manager.
John joins us from Linde Material Handling Australia, he currently runs Linde's Perth-based service department and is looking to head back to New Zealand to be closer to his grandchildren. John is originally from New Zealand and is looking forward to getting home. We are extremely excited to have John join the LDX team and bring his years of experience in the material handling space.

It's an exciting time to be in business, it's exciting to create a new business unit and diversify into an entirely new area complementary to TDX.

It will be an amazing year ahead for TDX and especially LDX and the new team.

Cheers,
Joe.

The LDX Journey





AUGUST 23

Company registration of LDX Ltd and trademark application completed.

SEPTEMBER 23

The dealer agreement was worked through and signed with Linde in late September.

OCTOBER 23

Recruitment efforts were also underway, with more than 14 roles advertised. Order planning for inventory, parts, and vans was completed. A review of dealer management systems was completed and the decision to adopt the Orion ERP system was made.

NOVEMBER 23

The start of a marketing campaign marked the launch of LDX, this included building the website, business cards, logs and all of those things that go with starting something up from scratch. Additionally, an LMH Australian technician was stationed in Auckland. Operations were streamlined with new vans, laptops, and phones set up and ready for technicians.

The IT infrastructure was established and included the setting up of a domain, emails, form tab integration, Salesforce, and call flow planning. An interim quoting process was implemented. Meanwhile, training sessions for the Orion ERP system were underway, ensuring smooth integration and proficiency among staff.

DECEMBER 23

Three Technicians and an administrator were recruited.

JANUARY 24

The Orion ERP system went live on January 16, marking a significant milestone in the implementation process. A North Island Territory Manager was recruited and appointed, enhancing managerial capabilities in the region. These developments enhanced operational efficiency and expanded managerial capacity within LDX. As well as all of the above we also had all of the LDX team together to complete a good robust induction to the Linde product, this included Linde Material handling staff from Australia to ensure that all of our team were well equipped.

FEBRUARY 24

Operations Manager recruited and started at the end of February.

Linde's NZ-based parts stock is in place. As well as New forklift stock that was in the country, we also have placed our own stock order of new equipment that is currently destined for New Zealand.

Over ONE MILLION dollars of quotes produced for new forklift enquiries.

To come... MARCH 24

General Manager, and Service Manager recruited and starting March.

Fully maintained service agreement to be signed off and in place for all Linde Material Handling equipment currently in New Zealand, this equates to around 300 forklifts.

 

Linde MH Australia and LDX: The start of a perfect trans-Tasman partnership



As part of the launch of the new LDX business, we had a chat with Leigh Atallah, Director of Sales and Marketing for Linde Material Handling Australia.

The term 'a match made in heaven' isn't used all that often in business, but when it comes to Linde Material Handling and the new partnership with TDX and the establishment of LDX, there's every reason it should be.

We've been speaking with the TDX team for a number of months and have been thoroughly impressed with their professionalism and dedication to getting the new business up and running. However, of even greater importance and something we really respect, are the synergies which exist between our two businesses - especially when it comes to our culture and values.

These shared values have provided a solid base on which to build the new business and will provide exciting avenues for growth and expansion.

We're confident we will be able to work together and achieve significant results. This is thanks to LDX having a nationwide footprint, as well as a strong and loyal customer base through the larger TDX operation.

We know there will be outstanding growth opportunities for Linde MH products to find a home with TDX customers.

With our three-tier product range - Performance plus, Performance and Value (fully available to the NZ market) - Linde MH and Baoli will provide LDX access to a material handling solution that meets almost every customer application, from the harshest of environments to light use applications.

Sustainability is a key driver for our NZ customer base and Linde MH is a leader in electrification (the switch from internal combustion to electric powered equipment). Electrification is a Mega trend within the global material handling market, driving reduced CO2 emissions. Through our partnership with LDX, our customers will have access to the largest range of lithium-ion batteries and chargers, enabling electrification and driving sustainably.


We're excited to have LDX on board. We know they will be a great partner and will represent our brand well. We're keen on the team; now it's up to all of us to make it happen.
